# Shoot Gravitropism Core Hypothesis

Status: hypothesis-level seed idea, not curated biology.

## Plain-English Hypothesis

Shoot gravitropism begins when a shoot is rotated relative to gravity. Gravity
sensing tissues, including statocytes and the endodermis, detect the new
orientation. Amyloplast sedimentation is hypothesised to help convert the
physical stimulus into a biological signal.

That signal is hypothesised to pass through a LAZY/LZY-related module, which
affects PIN-mediated auxin redistribution. Auxin then becomes asymmetric across
the shoot. This auxin asymmetry is hypothesised to create differential cell
expansion, causing curvature. Over time, curvature produces the observed shoot
gravitropic bending phenotype.

Evolutionary variation may enter the system through family-level or genotype
effects. These effects could change sensing, signalling, auxin redistribution,
growth response, or the final bending trajectory. Some lineages or families may
therefore be more strongly gravitropic, weakly gravitropic, or agravitropic.

## Working Layers

1. Stimulus
   - gravistimulation or rotation

2. Sensing
   - statocytes
   - endodermis
   - amyloplast sedimentation

3. Signalling
   - LAZY/LZY module
   - PIN-mediated auxin redistribution

4. Response
   - auxin asymmetry
   - differential cell expansion
   - curvature

5. Phenotype
   - shoot gravitropic bending over time

6. Evolutionary layer
   - family or genotype effects
   - gravitropic versus agravitropic families

## What This Hypothesis Does Not Claim Yet

- It does not claim that any edge is curated.
- It does not claim that every edge is a direct molecular mechanism.
- It does not identify a final species scope.
- It does not yet distinguish early sensing dynamics from later developmental
  response.
- It does not yet separate conserved mechanisms from lineage-specific
  evolutionary changes.
