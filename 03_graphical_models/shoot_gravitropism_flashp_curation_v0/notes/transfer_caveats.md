# Transfer Caveats

Arabidopsis is useful as a mechanistic prior for plant shoot gravitropism, but it is not direct evidence for `Senecio lautus`.

## Main Caveats

1. Species context differs.

Arabidopsis evidence may involve seedlings, hypocotyls, inflorescence stems, or roots. The target project concerns `Senecio lautus` shoot gravitropic bending and family-level comparisons.

2. Tissue and stage may differ.

Evidence from Arabidopsis hypocotyls or inflorescence stems may not map cleanly to the tissues used in the `Senecio` dark-rotation experiment.

3. Timescale may differ.

The target experimental context is an 8-hour dark rotation response. Some seed references may support mechanism but not that exact timescale.

4. Evolutionary interpretation requires direct evidence.

Arabidopsis cannot support claims about gravitropic versus agravitropic `Senecio` families without direct comparative evidence.

5. Mechanistic refinement may be needed.

Broad v0.1 nodes such as `LAZY_LZY_Module` and `PIN_Auxin_Redistribution` may need to be split into gene-specific or process-specific nodes after evidence review.

## Practical Rule

When in doubt, write:

```text
Arabidopsis mechanistic prior only; Senecio transfer untested.
```
